PowerChart — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 5 July 2026

PowerChart is an open-source PowerPoint add-in that turns chart definitions into native, editable PowerPoint shapes. It is designed to keep your data with you.

What data PowerChart handles

The chart data and settings you enter are processed entirely inside your PowerPoint client to draw shapes on your slide. PowerChart persists a chart's configuration in a shape tag inside your own document (so a chart can be re-edited later) and stores your saved templates and style defaults in your browser's localStorage on your device.

What we do not do

  • We do not transmit your slides, chart data, or documents to any server we control.
  • We do not use accounts, logins, analytics, advertising, or tracking cookies.
  • We do not sell or share any personal information — we don't collect any.

Hosting

The add-in's static files (scripts, icons, this page) are served over HTTPS from powerchart.struktureretsundfornuft.dk, hosted on GitHub Pages. As with any website, the host may record standard technical request logs (e.g. IP address, timestamp) for security and reliability. PowerChart itself adds no additional collection on top of that.

Third-party services

Inside PowerPoint, the add-in loads Microsoft's Office.js library from Microsoft's content delivery network, and runs within the Microsoft Office platform, which is governed by Microsoft's own privacy terms.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised at the project's public repository: github.com/dannbleeker/PowerChart.
